Latitude vs. Temperature

Statistical analysis:

  • The value of r-squared was 0.559308446073326.
  • The line equation for linear regression was: y = -0.6x + 67.0.

Maximum temperature reaches the highest values around the 0 degree latitutde. This is to be expected since it is common knowledge that temperatures increase as we near the equator. However, the plot is not a perfect bell curve. The maximum temperatures on the left side of the graph (negative latitude) drop less dramatically than the right side of the graph (postive latitude). We can also tell by the range of the x-axis and the concentration of data points that there were more randomly generated cities north of the equator.
